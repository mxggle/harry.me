I always believe that if we want to remember things quickly, we need to connect new knowledge to our existing memories. Existing memories are like fertile soil—they nourish new ideas, helping them take root and grow easily.

Our memories shape who we are, and they take many forms. In this article, I want to explore one specific form: **our hobbies**. Think of it like signing up for a news app—it asks what topics interest you, then customizes your feed accordingly. Because every article matches your interests, you stay engaged much longer. Language learning can work the same way.

Hobbies are not just what we do for fun — they are **emotional gateways to memory**. When we learn something that connects to what we already love, our brains light up. Emotion strengthens memory, and familiarity provides context. That’s why a word related to your passion — whether it’s coffee, photography, or coding — sticks faster than one that feels distant. In short, hobbies aren’t just interests; they’re **shortcuts to remembering**.

So how can we connect our hobbies to Japanese vocabulary?

The traditional way is to immerse yourself in real Japanese environments: watching anime, listening to J-pop, reading manga, or joining Reddit communities about your interests. But with AI, you can now create that personalized immersion instantly—with just a prompt.

Here’s an example of one that works beautifully:

> > **You are a native Japanese speaker, a creative language teacher, and \[Your name\]’s close Japanese friend who deeply understands his life.**\*\*
> > 
> > **You know he lives in \[Your city\], loves \[Your hobbies\]; he’s studying Japanese and enjoys natural, emotional expressions.**
> > 
> > **Generate 10–15 natural Japanese example sentences for the given word.**  
> > **Each sentence should reflect his life and interests**
> > 
> > **Each example includes two lines:**
> > 
> > 1. Japanese sentence
> > 
> > 3. English translation
> > 
> > Make all sentences natural, realistic, and emotionally resonant — like real moments from \[Your Name\]’s life.

This prompt is designed to make AI generate **personalized Japanese example sentences** that feel emotionally and contextually connected to _you_.

And this is my Prompt 

> > **You are a native Japanese speaker, a creative language teacher, and Harry’s close Japanese friend who deeply understands his life.**
> > 
> > **You know he lives in Tokyo, loves coffee, photography, coding, and AI; he’s studying Japanese and enjoys natural, emotional expressions.**
> > 
> > **Generate 10–15 natural Japanese example sentences for the given word.**  
> > **Each sentence should reflect his life and interests**
> > 
> > **Each example includes two lines:**
> > 
> > 1. Japanese sentence
> > 
> > 3. English translation
> > 
> > Make all sentences natural, realistic, and emotionally resonant — like real moments from Harry’s life.

Here are a few examples generated for the word **乱れる (みだれる)**:

1. 髪が風で乱れてしまったけど、そのままの方が自然で好きだ。  
    _My hair got messy in the wind, but I actually like it that way—it feels natural._

3. 心が乱れているときは、コーヒーを淹れて深呼吸するようにしている。  
    _When my mind feels unsettled, I make coffee and take a deep breath._

5. 雨の中で撮った写真は、光が乱れて幻想的だった。  
    _The photos I took in the rain had scattered light that looked almost magical._

7. 猫が夜中に走り回って、寝るリズムがすっかり乱れた。  
    _The cat ran around at night and completely messed up my sleep rhythm._

9. 桜の花びらが風に乱れる様子は、どこか儚くて美しい。  
    _The sight of cherry blossoms scattered by the wind is fleetingly beautiful._

11. AIの返答が遅れると、ちょっと気持ちが乱れる。  
     When the AI’s response is delayed, it makes me a bit uneasy.

13. 長く座ってコードを書いていると、姿勢が乱れて体が固くなる。  
     When I sit coding too long, my posture slips and my body stiffens.

15. 試験の前は、いつも心拍が少し乱れる。  
     Before an exam, my heartbeat always gets a little irregular.

The results were incredible. Every sentence felt personal, like a note from an old friend.  
Each sentence is not just an example—it’s a **memory anchor**. When I think of “乱れる” now, I don’t recall a dictionary definition; I recall my cat, coffee, the wind in Tokyo, and a photo I once took in the rain.  
And don’t forget—AI like ChatGPT has its own memory, too. That memory is like a fragment of your own, reflecting pieces of who you are ——— This makes each example relatable and easy to recall.

That’s the magic of connecting language learning with who you are.

By making AI part of your memory ecosystem, you can turn every new word into a familiar friend.

So….Next time you learn a new word, let AI write your life into it.”
